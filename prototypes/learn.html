Inversion

Inversion is a powerful thinking tool where instead of focusing on what you want to achieve, you consider what you want to avoid.

By thinking backward from failure, you can identify and eliminate obstacles before they derail your progress. Charlie Munger famously said, "All I want to know is where I'm going to die, so I'll never go there." This captures the essence of inversion thinking.

When applied to procrastination, instead of asking "How can I be more productive?" you ask "What would guarantee I procrastinate?" Then, you systematically avoid those triggers.

Example in Practice

"If I wanted to ensure I never finish my report, I would: keep my phone on my desk, work with 15 browser tabs open, and start without a clear outline. Therefore, I'll do the opposite: phone in another room, only relevant tabs open, and spend 5 minutes outlining first."

This approach works because it's often easier to see what causes failure than to predict what guarantees success. By inverting the problem, you create a practical checklist of pitfalls to avoid.
